Using Machine Learning to Predict Breast Cancer

August 14, 2025

Breast cancer remains one of the most prevalent and life-threatening diseases worldwide. In this post, I explore how machine learning models like Random Forest and Logistic Regression can be used to classify tumors based on clinical features. By training on publicly available datasets, I achieved over 95% accuracy in distinguishing between benign and malignant cases.

The project involved data preprocessing, feature selection, and hyperparameter tuning. Visualizations using Matplotlib helped interpret model performance and feature importance. This work not only demonstrates the power of predictive analytics in healthcare but also highlights the importance of explainability in medical AI.
